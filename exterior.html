The exterior finishes may be detailed within the planning consent and will be part of the architect’s design. Cost savings can be achieved if necessary subject to planning consent without compromising the design too much, but this exercise should be undertaken with the help and advice of your architect and Quantity Surveyor. Think about durability and maintenance as well as aesthetics.

Stonework

  • Real
  • Artificial

Real Stone

Natural or Real stone is quarried and often imported increasing the carbon footprint. The choice between natural and cast stone products comes down to personal preference and the constraints of the project, such as time-scales and budgets.

Artificial Stone

Reconstituted stone is produced in a mould using cement, limestone, pigments and additives and the finished product closely replicates the natural stone alternative.

Render

  • Cement
  • Lime
  • Polymer
  • Monocouche
  • Insulated
  • Acrylic

Cement Render

This is a cement based one-coat top layer render and needs to be painted.

Lime Render

Lime render is flexible and breathable and has a certain finish increasingly sought after.

Polymer Render

Polymer ingredients are used to bind this type of render. It can be pre-coloured with near enough any pigment to produce an individual finish.

Monocouche Render

This uses cement as a hardening agent, two coats are recommended. It comes in 19 standard through-colours and can be finished smooth or textured to look like stone.

Insulated Render

Insulated Render usually refers to an external wall insulation system that not only contains a coloured render, but an insulation material that is adhered to the underlying layer.

Acrylic Render

Acrylic ingredients are used to bind this type of render. It tends to be pre-made, arriving on site in tubs – It’s durable, but it can attract algae.

Roof Covering

  • Concrete
  • Artificial Slate
  • Plain
  • Natural Slate
  • Welsh Slate
  • 3 Layer Felt
  • Poly - EDPM
  • Sarnafil

Concrete

The most popular choice; it gives a low-maintenance finish. These tiles are fire resistant too which will reduce the damage a fire could do to your property.

Artificial Slate

Synthetic roof tile with the look of slate but lighter – doesn’t break.

Plain

Suitable for traditional designs in a wide range of colours.

Natural Slate

Slate has a characteristic appearance and it has extreme durability, with experts believing a well-maintained slate roof can last up to 150 years. More sustainable if reused.

Welsh Slate

Expensive but gives an authentic look to period properties.

3 Layer Felt

Roofing system for flat roofs.

Poly - EDPM

A durable synthetic rubber roofing membrane.

Sarnafil

Fiberglass-reinforced, single-ply membrane.

Rainwater Goods

  • Plastic
  • Aluminium
  • Cast Iron

Plastic

Durable and light weight low cost pipes and gutters to deal with rainwater on the property.

Aluminium

Lightweight and does not rust; mid range price.

Cast Iron

Longer lasting than other options; often chosen for period properties.

UPVC

The cheapest option; durable and easy to maintain.

Aluminium

Modern styling and long lasting; may not be the most efficient option.

Crittal

Crittal style refers to black steel or aluminium frames with glass panels; now fire rated, double glazed and thermally efficient.

Fineline

Ultra-slim aluminium windows and doors.

Softwood

Paintable and better looking for traditional builds.

Hardwood

Traditionally used in high end traditional and period properties.

Steel Frame

Strong and secure windows and doors.

Velux - Standard

A leading brand of roof window.

Velux - Electric

Roof window with electrical controls.

Velux - Conservation

Conservation roof windows – traditionally clad in lead, bespoke made.
