Durability and maintenance are a factor in high traffic areas, but one of the main considerations in choosing a floor finish is lifestyle. Pets and an outdoor lifestyle mean durable and easily cleaned surfaces might be preferable.

Carpet

Affordable but not durable or suitable for all rooms.

Wood Laminate

Needs a flat surface and looks like hardwood or stone; not as durable.

Engineered Wood Flooring

Combines multiple layers of timber with a solid wood veneer. Designed to allow for wood expansion, engineered wood is compatible with underfloor heating systems and has a high moisture tolerance level.

Ceramic

Glazed tiles are durable, decorative and suitable for many settings including kitchens and bathrooms.

Porcelain

Porcelain tiles can look like stone, slate or wood.

Large Format

Large format tiles give the illusion of space; they are made of porcelain.

Marble

Marble is luxurious but requires maintenance and can be costly.

Stone

A natural look which requires upkeep and maintenance.
