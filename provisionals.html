Provisional Sums

  • Electrical Specification
  • Electrical Fittings

Provisional sums can be defined or undefined sums in the construction contract and is a way to deal with cost items which may not be known or specified at the time of the initial budget planning. Contingency provision is an example of a provisional sum. It is a useful way to share risk between the client and contractor. In the electrical section a provisional sum might be for a home automation, security or a lighting and sound system.

If something like this is on your wish list, then ask your DGA Quantity Surveyor for a guide price until you get a reliable quotation from a contractor.

Electrical Specification

  • NHBC Required
  • Mid Range
  • Super Home

DGA would always advise asking your architect for an electrical layout drawing so that the specification can be worked up early. Changes during the project or at the end including socket and switch positions and numbers can be disproportionately expensive.

NHBC Required

Basic specification - low cost. This is the entry level safe specification provided by NHBC - more details can be found on the NHBC website. Here is an example of what this level of specification would include.

Mid Range

Mid range electrical layouts. This specification would allow for a more sophisticated wiring design with perhaps underfloor heating or some level of home automations and AV systems.

Super Home

This top of the range specification would be capable of supporting technology for controlled heating, air conditioning, lighting, security and audio visual systems.
